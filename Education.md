# Education Analysis

## Aim
To examine how educational systems may contribute to mental health pressure.

## Overview
This section of the project focuses on the role of educational institutions in shaping mental health outcomes. It explores how factors such as academic pressure, workload, and access to support services may influence student wellbeing.

## Dataset Description
This analysis uses data relating to:
- Student mental health prevalence (e.g. anxiety, depression rates)
- Demand for university mental health support services
- Indicators of academic pressure where available

Primary data sources:
- UK Office for National Statistics (ONS)
- University mental health service data

Supporting sources:
- Academic research on student wellbeing
- Reports on university counselling demand

## Methodology
The analysis follows a structured approach:
- Data collection and preparation
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Visualisation of trends over time
- Interpretation of findings in a real-world context

## Planned Visualisation
- Line graph showing changes in mental health trends over time  
- Comparison of support service demand where applicable  

## Key Insight (In Progress)
Initial analysis suggests a potential increase in student mental health concerns over time. This may indicate growing psychological pressure within educational environments, particularly in relation to academic demands and access to support services.

## Limitations
- Data availability may vary across sources
- Some datasets may not directly measure academic pressure
- Findings may indicate correlation rather than causation
