# Healthcare Analysis

## Aim
To examine whether individuals with different mental health and neurodevelopmental conditions experience unequal access to NHS support, and to explore how healthcare systems may create additional pressure through differences in waiting times, service availability, and treatment pathways.

This analysis investigates whether support appears proportional to demand and explores the factors that may contribute to disparities in access across conditions.

## Overview
This section of the project explores whether the healthcare system creates unequal pressure depending on the mental health or neurodevelopmental condition an individual experiences.

While mental health conditions are often discussed collectively, access to support, waiting times, treatment pathways, and service availability can vary significantly between conditions. Factors such as prevalence, clinical complexity, specialist workforce requirements, funding allocation, and service design may influence how quickly individuals receive assessment and treatment.

This analysis compares a range of conditions, including anxiety, depression, obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD), Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), autism spectrum disorder (ASD), borderline personality disorder (BPD), and schizophrenia, to investigate whether some groups face greater barriers to care than others.

By examining differences in demand, access, waiting times, and service provision, this project aims to identify where healthcare systems may unintentionally create additional pressure for individuals seeking support. It also explores whether the level of support available appears proportional to the demand and complexity associated with each condition.

Ultimately, this analysis seeks to understand how healthcare structures may contribute to unequal experiences of care and whether certain conditions are better supported than others within the NHS.
